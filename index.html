OUR VALUES

  • Integrity We are committed to upholding the highest standards of integrity in everything we do. Each member of our brigade is expected to act with honesty, transparency, and accountability. We believe that trust is the foundation of our strength, and we aim to earn and maintain the trust of our fellow soldiers, commanders, and the people we serve.

  • Courage Courage is the cornerstone of our operations. We face challenges with determination, overcoming fear and uncertainty in the pursuit of our mission. Whether on the battlefield or in our daily responsibilities, we remain steadfast, ready to make tough decisions and take bold actions when necessary.

  • Loyalty Loyalty is not just a word—it's a commitment. We are unwavering in our dedication to our comrades, our unit, and our mission. We stand united, supporting one another through difficult times, and ensuring the success of our collective goals. Our loyalty extends beyond duty; it’s a bond built on mutual respect and shared purpose.

  • Discipline Discipline is the foundation of our operational effectiveness. We uphold rigorous standards of training, conduct, and performance. Through discipline, we ensure that every action is executed with precision and efficiency. It is through discipline that we maintain order, respect, and readiness at all times.

  • Honor Our actions reflect our honor. We uphold the values of courage, integrity, and respect in everything we do. Every decision, whether on or off duty, is guided by a sense of duty to our country and the trust placed in us. Our honor is earned through dedication to our service, and we strive to uphold it in every interaction, mission, and challenge.

  • Excellence Striving for excellence is at the core of our mission. We constantly seek to improve and refine our skills, knowledge, and strategies. Whether in training, operations, or leadership, we aim to exceed expectations and set new standards for ourselves and others. Excellence is not an option; it is a requirement that drives us forward.

FIND YOUR PLACE

We are launching a trial week at our recruitment center

1. How can I join the brigade?

To join the Lead Rain Assault Brigade, you need to complete the online application form on our website. After submitting your details, you'll be contacted by our recruitment team to schedule an interview and physical fitness assessment. Additionally, you may be required to participate in a trial week to ensure you're fit for service.

2. What are the requirements to join?

To be eligible for service in the brigade, you must meet the following criteria: Be a citizen of the country (or a foreign volunteer with legal documentation). Be physically fit and healthy. Be between the ages of 6 and 40 (exceptions may apply based on prior military experience). Successfully pass a background check and security screening. Complete the necessary physical and psychological evaluations.

3. Do I need military experience to join?

No, prior military experience is not required to join the Lead Rain Assault Brigade. We offer comprehensive training programs to prepare recruits for the challenges they will face in service. What we look for are strong values, physical fitness, and the will to serve.

4. Is there any training provided?

Yes, training is a vital part of joining the brigade. All recruits undergo basic training, followed by specialized courses based on their role. Training includes physical conditioning, weapons handling, tactical exercises, and leadership development. Our goal is to ensure that every member is well-prepared to serve.

5. What is the "trial week"?

The "trial week" is an introductory period designed for potential recruits to experience the physical and mental demands of service. It allows you to assess whether military life is right for you, while our team evaluates your fitness and readiness. During this week, you'll participate in basic physical exercises and live the daily routine of a soldier.
